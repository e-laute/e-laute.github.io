E-LAUTE at the Long Night of Research 2026

Experience music history up close! The E-LAUTE research project invites you to the Long Night of Research 2026 – featuring interactive stations, fascinating insights into historical notation, and unique sonic experiences.

📍 Two locations in Vienna – twice the opportunity to explore, experiment, and be amazed!

🟥 University of Vienna – Hands-on Station

Try it yourself: Discover lute tablature

Dive into the fascinating world of historical music notation!
At our hands-on station in the main building of the University of Vienna, you can get involved yourself:

  • Try out notation directly on a laptop
  • Create your own lute tablatures using creative symbols
  • Handle facsimiles of valuable manuscripts and prints from the Austrian National Library

When?: 24.04.2026, 17:00–22:45
Where?: Main Building, University of Vienna, Universitätsring 1, 1010 Vienna

Experience how music was recorded centuries ago – interactive and hands-on!

🟥 mdw – Workshop

Listen, Read, Understand: Rediscover Music

At the mdw campus, an interactive workshop awaits you, centered on the E-LAUTE digital edition platform:

  • Learn to read previously hard-to-access music in German lute tablature
  • Listen to these works performed on historical instruments
  • Discover how digital research opens up musical cultural heritage

When?: 24.04.2026, Starting times: 18:30, 19:30, 20:30, 21:30
Participants: max. 10 per session
Where?: mdw – University of Music and Performing Arts Vienna, Anton-von-Webern-Platz 1, 1030 Vienna

Registration required by April 19, 2026: stojak@mdw.ac.at
(Please include your name and preferred starting time)
